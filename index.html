Challenges

The technological advancements of the modern world have, as a byproduct, generated a tremendous amount of data from people all around the globe. Internet of things, social media, personal devices: everything connected to the internet can gather information about the habits and trends of the people and. This data is becoming an extremely valuable asset used by businesses to identify the correct customer base and adapt the products accordingly.

Research

In majority of the cases the people producing the data do not have control over how it is used by businesses. Data marketplace is a solution that addresses that problem by allowing users to control how, when and how much their data is used. A data marketplace is a place to connect the producers of data (people, in some cases other businesses) with businesses that can put the data to use. While there exist other types of data marketplaces, the focus of this project is a subcategory called the personal data marketplace:

A following set of personal data marketplaces already exists:

  • Datum
  • Datawallet
  • Fysical

The major issue with data marketplaces is a concern for the privacy of the user providing the data. Depending on the country of use, various privacy acts must be respected, such as CCPA5 (California Consumer Privacy Act), GDPR6 (General Data Protection Regulation) and Quebec Privacy Act7.

Marketplaces like Datum, Datawallet and Fysical leverage the recent coming of the blockchain protocol in order to ensure that the user's data is only accessible by the intended recipient.

A quick overview of the mobile solutions has revealed the following problems with the applications interfacing the market: Inspection of the Datum mobile solutions has discovered that the user registration functionality of the app is compromised, making the application receive bad reviews on the PlayStore and AppStore:

Datawallet website was largely confusing and felt like the product offered by the company is a compliance training, rather than the data market solution.

Fysical has had a pleasing and intuitive website, explaining the use cases and the scope of the application. However, fysical is only targeting the location data as its product, leaving out a huge swath of other possible data out.

Overall, it looks like the data market solutions offered at the moment are either ineffective in the customer facing delivery (Datum), unclear in its objective (datawallet) or have a very narrow scope of what data can be sold (Fysical).

Design Decisions


Website actionable content

Based on the research and survey conducted by us, the following criteria were deemed to be most important when creating the market:
First and foremost, security: we have proposed a multi factor authentication as a step to ensure the security of the account
Next, the strict control over one‚Äôs data. To facilitate that, we have created a data source and a market page with allowed sources and buyers respectively, to present a two-layer insurance that sensitive data doesn‚Äôt fall in the wrong hands
In addition, we have created a ‚Äústop all data‚Äù button to be able to pause data flow in order to give the user another layer of control
We have also added a finance page for people more interested in the income aspect of the trade, where one can view their income generation and transfer the accumulated payments to a bank account of their choice
Last, but not least, we have chosen to restrict the data types to a limited set, in order to make sure that the users don‚Äôt give away some type of sensitive data, like financial (if the user doesn‚Äôt want to share it)


Functional structure

We have decided to go with the principle: less is more. This decision manifested itself in similar patterns of pages, with recycled visual elements and similar actions. A minimalistic card structure, reused both for data sources and market kept the UI lean, mean and clean.


Artistic flair

What is design without art?
Although it was difficult to integrate some kind of artistic expression into a technology-focused website, we have tried our best. Alluring phrases (one phrase, to be honest) of the written art, adorable representations of business logos, contrasts chosen, everything came from the minds and souls of our best artists.


Color

With a little bit of research, we have decided to partially lean on the concepts of material design when choosing our colour schemes and accents, specifically in the color gammas we have tried to apply.
After some meddling around we have selected a total of 3 palette gammas, presented below:


While all the palettes are harmonious (thanks to material.io), we have decided that the Cool blue palette evokes the emotions of technology, data and security. For the background, the charcoal dark was chosen to keep the eyes relaxed and not overload the user.


Fonts

For the buttons and some other elements of the website, we have chosen the Roboto. It is clean, it is widespread, it is easy to read.


With the use of Google fonts, we have found Ubuntu for the majority of our buttons. It also feels clean and simplistic. The slight curve in the letters fit rather well with the smooth edges of the buttons in our design.
